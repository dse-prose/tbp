DSE mockup

The following sections present a custom solution for the development of a digital scholarly edition, based on the specific characteristics of the case study outlined in the Note and the encoding strategies discussed in the XML/TEI encoding section. First of all, from a technical perspective, a custom solution is required due to the use of the double endpoint attachment method for encoding textual variants, which necessitates specific handling in the transformation process. As for editorial goals, the primary aim is to enable comparison between the first printed edition of Bembo’s treatise, the earlier version documented by manuscript Vat.lat. 3210 (V), and the subsequent printed editions (M and T). In line with the principles of the printed critical edition (Note, Bembo 2001), the internal elaboration of the manuscript text is also taken into account.

To this end, the edition will offer multiple textual reconstructions derived from the TEI-encoded source file. For the manuscript witness, three separate outputs are envisioned: base draft, final text, and revision, which documents the sequence of authorial interventions. Each printed witness will likewise be transformed into a separate document.

The interface design is intended to reflect the authorial process, distinguishing the phase leading from the manuscript to the editio princeps from that which continues from the princeps to the final edition. The core functionality, referred to as the parallel view, aims to facilitate comparison between the text of P and that of the other witnesses, while also documenting the elaborative process related to the manuscript and the variants at the level of each individual witness. A complementary single view mode allows for a focused reading of each witness, accompanied by facsimile images where available.
In addition, the proposal includes general functionalities related to other content elements included in the encoding model, such as quotations, linguistic annotations, and other metadata.

Parallel view

As explained in the Note, two moments can be identified in the history of Bembo’s Prose and the princeps can be considered as a link between them. The compositional process has been reflected in the interface, which enables the comparison between the text of the princeps and, alternatively, the one of the manuscript witness and of subsequent printed editions.

In the parallel view, the text is presented in scroll-down mode, with page numbers embedded in the text. Segments involved in variants are highlighted with the same background color. In order to avoid clutter, it would be a choice of the user to highlight paragraphemic variants via the button on the footer of the page.

The printed critical edition chosen as starting point for the development of the project (see Bembo 2001) records the elaboration within the manuscript as well as the variant readings between the text documented by Vat.lat 3210 and the text documented by the princeps. In order to represent the diachrony of interventions within the manuscript, the mockup proposes three different reconstructions: base draft, with related immediate variants, final text, which presents the text resulting from the application of authorial corrections, finally, revision, which shows the corrections documented by the manuscript and referred to different layers of stratification.

The various typologies of variants within the manuscript are graphically represented as follows:

  • Deletions are represented with a strikethrough
  • Additions are represented as superscript text
  • Deletions and additions which can be explained as part of the same intervention are represented as deletionaddition
  • When it was possible to distinguish more phases within an elaborated passage, the reconstructed texts are represented one after the other, preceded by a number which specifies the correct sequence
  • Elaborated passages can be identified by a blue color font. Differences in ink and ductus have been recording in the encoding as explained in the dedicated section, and could eventually be displayed as popup information.
  • Based on the selected chapters, in case of transpositions within V, the variants occur between the base draft and the text in its subsequent elaboration and, also, between the base draft of the manuscript and the text of P. The latter case is thoroughly explained in the following paragraph, devoted to the representation of variants across witnesses, while transpositions within the witness are addressed as follows: the base draft shows the text in its original location, while the revision shows it in its new location, which is treated as a link connected to the original location of the text, now replaced by a placeholder.

Finally, errors listed in the Errata corrige of each edition can be identified by a bright red font color.

The reason for this macro-division between base draft, revision and final text is both due to the prosaic nature of the text and the correction typologies documented by the history of Prose, which, especially from the princeps onward, are mostly additions and transpositions. An edition which analytically reconstructs the correctional phases identifiable in the elaboration of the text could be hard to interpret for a case study such as the one at hand, whereas chronological encodingand phase visualization, if limited to the moments of base draft, final version and revision, including the representation of the eventual internal stratification in the latter, seem more immediate and readable, without excluding the representation of the diachrony of the text, and also appear to be sound with the ecdotic principles of the printed edition.

As for variants across witnesses, the following cases have been addressed:

  • Omissions are identified by a red background color
  • Additions are identified by a green background color
  • Transpositions share a purple background color. If there is a one-to-one correspondence between the original passage and the transposed one, transpositions would be treated as links from P to the target. In cases where a single original passage corresponds to multiple transposed passages across different witnesses, a possible solution could be to display a tooltip containing the actual links to each transposed passage.

Finally, the model takes into account stop-press corrections in the third edition. These are written in bold and underlined. By clicking on the portion of text involved in the correction, a modal would allow to read the text prior to the corrections and, possibly, the copy from which the text is derived.

A basic working prototype in JavaScript has been realized to show the main features of the Parallel view. For the implementation of the prototype, the output texts for the witnesses and textual reconstructions of the manuscript have been manually coded in HTML. However, if the DSE proposed in this section were to be implemented, multiple output HTML documents would be produced from the source XML/TEI, to be displayed in a JS based web application. Single view

The single view, which can be toggled for each witness, would provide the user the possibility to read the text next to the facsimiles of the respective edition. In the single view, the critical edition would display one page at a time, in order to facilitate the comparison between the transcription and the primary source. Errors listed in the Errata corrige would still be identifiable by a red font color, with a tooltip displaying the original text.

As for the manuscript witness, the single view would allow a comparison between each textual reconstruction and the facsimiles. In addition, a Text-to-text mode would provide the user the possibility to compare two reconstructions at a time.

Other features

For each witness included in the edition, a description has been provided in the XML/TEI file. The information associated with each witness would be displayed in an appropriate overlay:

On the content level, Named Entities and quotations were also encoded.
A button would allow the user to highlight the Named Entitiesencoded in the XML/TEI file. Hovering on an entity, a popup would show the normalized form of the name, a link to the authority control (VIAF or WikiData) and to the Dizionario Biografico degli Italiani entry where available. The full index of people recorded in the XML/TEI file would be accessible both from the lists button on the left and from the link provided in the popups.

Quotations are indented and typographically distinguished by a different font and italic style. On hover, a modal would display the quoted locus. For all quotations, the full index recorded in the XML/TEI file would be accessible both via the "Lists" button on the left and through the link provided in the popups.

The button in the top left corner opens the lists of Named Entities and other phenomena encoded in the XML/TEI file, in particular: people, sample words and quoted works. Each entry would be followed by the occurrences recorded for each witness.
