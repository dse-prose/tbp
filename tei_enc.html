XML/TEI encoding

Structure

Bembo's Prose is a dialogue, divided into three books that correspond to the three evenings - from 10 to 12 december 1502 - through which the conversation between Carlo Bembo, Ercole Strozzi, Giuliano de' Medici and Federigo Fregoso spans. Since the 1960 edition of the dialogue by Carlo Dionisotti, each of the books was further organized in chapters, which were adopted by subsequent editors. Finally, the critical edition by Claudio Vela added a subdivision of chapters into commas. These structural elements were encoded as follows:

Feature XML/TEI
Book 
<div type="book" xml:id="book_3"></div>
Chapter 
<div type="chapter" xml:id="chapter_number"></div>
Comma 
<milestone unit="comma" xml:id="comma_chapNumber_comNumber">

Representation

This level involved the encoding of some of the visual features of the witnesses. Specifically, for each of the four witnesses, a <pb/> element has been used in order to mark the beginning of a new page, in combination with the attributes @edRef, to link the page to the related witness via the sigla defined in the <listWit> element, @n to specify the page number, designed as indicated below and, finally, a @facs to associate each page with the related image:

<pb edRef="#witness" n=“WitnessPagenumber" facs=”Witness_Pagenumber”/>

This visualization should also provide the possibility to align each line in the facsimiles with the corresponding line of the transcription. For this purpose, <lb/> elements are used to mark the beginning of each line in the text or in the apparatus:

<lb edRef="#P"/>

Content

As mentioned, Prose is a grammatical treatise in the form of a dialogue. In order to record the speakers throughout the conversation, dialogues are enclosed between said tags, where the attributes @who and @toWhom specify the people involved in the dialogue by the reference to the @xml:id associated to each <person> in the <listPerson> element.

Another relevant and consistent feature of Bembo’s Prose is represented by quotations from vernacular authors, which Bembo references in his attempt to define a language that could serve as a model for literary writing. Quotations are enclosed between quote tags, either in the base-text or, when involved in variants, within the apparatus. Each quotation is associated to an @xml:id and to a @source, defined in the <listBibl> element. Where possible, authority control records have been provided. The snippet below shows an example from the TEI document:

                <quote xml:id="quote_16P" source="#bibl_02f"><l>Ma noi sem peregrin, come voi sete</l></quote>

                
                <!--Dante, Commedia, Purgatorio 2-->
                <bibl xml:id="bibl_02f">
                    <author ref="#DA">Dante Alighieri</author>,
                    <title level="m" type="work" sameAs="http://viaf.org/viaf/181860567">Commedia</title>
                    <biblScope unit="cantica" sameAs="http://viaf.org/viaf/1323156012401149700009">Purgatorio</biblScope>
                    <biblScope unit="canto" sameAs="http://viaf.org/viaf/173971357">2</biblScope>
                    <citedRange unit="verse" n="Purg_2_63">63</citedRange>
                </bibl>

The numerous people mentioned in the dialogue are encoded via the element <persName> (or with <rs> in case of referencing strings), which refers to the xml:id associated to <person> elements in the <teiHeader>. Together with a URI from VIAF or Wikidata, when possible a link to the related resource from Dizionario Biografico degli Italiani has been provided through the ref attribute of the <persName> tag. Different <listPerson> elements have been provided in order to distinguish between characters of the dialogue, fictional and non-fictional characters, quoted authors.

Sample words listed by the author for the exemplification of grammar rules are marked in the text as <span>, where @ana refers to the @xml:id of a <category> within a <taxonomy> defined in the <teiHeader> and @type refers to the visual features of the word enclosed between <span> tags, as recorded in Vela's critical edition («marked» for words printed/written in uppercase, «unmarked» otherwise). The taxonomy is organized in three main categories (Nome, Verbo, Avverbio), which match the overall organization of the third book and can be further defined by inner <category> elements.

Finally, variants were encoded using the double end-point attachment method and an external apparatus, placed within a <standOff> element. <anchor> tags were used to indicate the start and end points of variants within the base-text. The resulting XML/TEI document is thus structured as follows:

                <TEI xmlns="http://www.tei-c.org/ns/1.0" >
                    <teiHeader>
                    </teiHeader>
                    <standOff>
                        <!--apparatus-->
                    </standOff>
                    <text>
                    </text>
                </TEI>

The encoding of variants involved multiple markup levels: textual, genetic and intratextual. All these levels were encoded in the XML/TEI file through multiple TEI modules, in particular textcrit for variants across witnesses and transcr for the genetic level related to the manuscript, whereas for errors the choice element was selected.

Textual level

It records the reading selected by the editor (<lem>) and variants across witnesses, both substantial and paragraphemic, distinguished in the apparatus via the @type (with value either "substantial" or "paragraphemic").
The printed edition that is the starting point for the project, as already mentioned, selects the text of the princeps (P) as base-text and compares it with the final reading of manuscript Vat.lat. 3210 (V). Since two distinct redactional phases can be distinguished in the history of the text - the one from V to P and then from P itself to T through M – the textual units of the printed edition have been compared also with M and T, which were then manually collated with P to identify the variant readings across the printed editions.

The most recurring substantive variants include additions, deletions and transpositions. The table below shows some examples from the text with the related XML/TEI markup.

<lem wit="#P #M #T">et dell'altre</lem>
<rdg wit="#V" change="#layer-0"></rdg>
</app>
 In the passage from V to P, some text is added, which is preserved in the following editions (M and T), thus listed in the @wit attribute of <lem>, while the <rdg> element is left empty

<rdg wit="#M #T"></rdg>
</app> 		The text of P is already present in the base draft of V, but it is later omitted in the second and third edition

 As shown in the snippet, T dislocates the <seg> already present in P, which is partially rewritten, after the addition introduced by M. The @corresp was used to specify a correspondence relationship between the two segments.

Given the importance of formal variants for a text like Bembo's Prose, the printed critical edition of the editio princeps recorded paragraphemic variants between the text of P and manuscript Vat.lat. 3210 as well. In this regard, the use of the double-endpoint attachment method has proven particularly effective, as it allows for a flat encoding of overlapping substantial and paragraphemic variants through the use of anchors in the base-text. To clarify this, an example from chapter III is presented below:

XML/TEI Description
Apparatus:
<app from="#chap3_com1_E3s" to="#chap3_com1_E3e" type="substantive">
<lem wit="#P #M #T">a voi, che Italiano siete,</lem>
<rdg wit="#V" change="#layer-0"><mod change="#layer-0" place="above">a voi, che Italiano siete,</mod></rdg>
</app>
<app from="#chap3_com1_E4s" to="#chap3_com1_E4e" type="paragraphemic">
<lem wit="#P #M #T">siete, a</lem>
<rdg wit="#V">siete a</rdg>
</app>
Text:
<anchor xml:id="chap3_com1_E3s"/>a voi, che Italiano <anchor xml:id="chap3_com1_E4s"/>siete,<anchor xml:id="chap3_com1_E3e"/> a<anchor xml:id="chap3_com1_E4e"/>
 In the snippet, the variant "a voi, che Italiano siete," is encoded first as substantial, as an insertion added by the author during the revision of the manuscript. The following paragraphemic variant starts within the segment interested by the substantial variant, it but ends afterwards.

In order these phenomena, the text of the printed critical edition was preserved in its paragraphemic asset. For this reason, the omographs analyzed by the editor (Vela 2001, pp. LXXIV-LXXVIII) have been linked to a dedicated <fs> (feature structure) in the XML/TEI file, in order to disambiguate them and eventually make the linguistic annotation visible to the reader.

Genetic level

On top of the variants between V and P, Vela’s edition also records the elaboration within V through the system of combined markers and apparatus entries introduced in the Philological note.
V is composed by different strata: a base draft with immediate variants and different layers of elaboration on top of the base draft.
Furthermore, unlike the first two books, the third book presents different drafting campaigns – where differences of ink and ductus can be identified – and some correctorial accidents (Bembo 2001, p. XXXI). In particular, limiting the analysis to ff. 91r-99v (III i 1 – III vii 18), the editor identified the following inks:

  • ff. 91-130: black ink = ink a
  • slip added between f. 93v and f. 94r (iii 3): different ductus and ink (referred to as "ink j" for the sake of the encoding)
  • f. 96v, r. II (iv 17 and 18): interlinear and aside-written additions in ink b [washed brown ink used for ff. 131-135]

Consequently, the model uses the <listChange> element to store the different layers identified within the manuscript (ink a = layer-0; ink b = layer-1; ink j = layer-2) and links them to the variants via the @change attribute within <mod> elements.
The text of the base draft is enclosed between <rdg> tags and variants referred to the base draft are represented via transcriptional elements such as <add> and <del> or, when deletions and additions can be regarded as a single intervention on the text, via the <subst> element. Everything which is the result of elaboration on top of the base draft is enclosed between <mod> tags linked to the related layer (in case of insertions, the <rdg> element will be empty, and the <mod> will store the text of the addition and, eventually, inner interventions). The table below shows some examples from the text:

<lem wit="#P #M #T">rassomigliarli</lem>
<rdg wit="#V" change="#layer-0">rassomigliargli<mod change="#layer-0" type="reuse">rassomigliarli</mod>
</rdg>
</app> 		The text of the base draft is enclosed in an <rdg> element, which contains a <mod> with the revised text. @type with value "reuse" has been used to indicate that the correction is derived from a previous reading (cfr. Italia-Raboni 2021, p. 61).
Corrections in the base draft and elaboration that leads to the same text of P <app from="#chap2_com2_E1s" to="#chap2_com2_E1e" type="substantive">
<lem wit="#P #M">a casa di mio Fratello</lem>

<rdg wit="#T">a casa mio Fratello</rdg>

This phenomenon has been encoded by assigning an xml:id to each segment involved in the transposition and cross-referencing them though a @corresp that points to the respective identifier
Intratextual level

This level concerns alternative variants, stop-press corrections and errors listed in the Errata corrige of the editions.
As mentioned in the Note, stop-press corrections are an important feature of the posthomous edition of Bembo's Prose. Both stop-press and alternative variants (though no example of the latter was found in the chapters selected for the project) were encoded by enclosing the variant in a span or seg and associating it an @ana which refers to a category in a dedicated taxonomy. Additionally, for stop-press corrections the responsible for the correction and the printed sources for the analysis were identified via @resp and @source respectively and the text which preceeds the correction is recorded via a note element.

<seg xml:id="spc-2" ana="#stop-press" source="#S-2001 #BCP-2018" resp="#BV">non si direbbon cosi spesso <sic>nelli</sic> prose come nel verso.<note target="#spc-2" type="T_variants" source="#T2">non si direbbono se non nel verso</note></seg>

The copy used as source for recording the text before Varchi's intervention has not been treated as a witness, but only described in a dedicated <msDesc> element. In the teiHeader, <relation> elements have been used to describe the relation between the copies of the editions of Prose and the editions themselves.

Errors are enclosed between <choice> tags, within which <sic> tags have been used to record the original text and <corr> tags record the corrected text. In order to distinguish corrections listed in the Errata corrige from those made by the editor, @resp has been used to identify the person responsible for the intervention, either Pietro Bembo or the editor of the critical edition.
